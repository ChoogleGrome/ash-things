New Social Closeness Guidelines: Prime Minister believes some social distancing practices are “un-Australian”

Other News

The Prime Minister has announced new guidelines that has also been approved by the Federal Health Department, as they believe that the global standard on Social Distancing is “un-Australian and rude”.

Social Distancing practices has been the forefront for the common public’s battle against the COVID-19 pandemic, in tandem with PPE usage.

“On new social distancing: stop avoiding handshakes. I can’t be more blunt about it. Stop it.,” The Prime Minister stated.

“It is not sensible, it is not helpful, and it has been one of the most disappointed things in response to this crisis.”

“I am seeking Australian’s common-sense cooperation with these very clear advisory decisions. Stop doing it. It’s ridiculous. Its un-Australian, and it must stop,” he said.

The new guidelines in question follows after multiple occasions where the Prime Minister is refused handshakes and hugs from citizens, the more recent being his visit to the Royal Adelaide Hospital. All 20 patients in the Intensive Care Unit (ICU) refused to shake hands with the Prime Minister.

The list of guidelines has been published on the Department of Health’s website, outlining 4 main guidelines surrounding handshakes and hugs primarily, as well as on indoor gatherings:

1. Handshaking should be accepted by everyone, as it is rude to decline. Police may enforce this guideline if conflict arises with a Member of the Parliament or other Authoritative Figure.
2. Hugging should be accepted if citizen does not show symptoms of COVI-19, however, to be safe, all hugs should be accepted to avoid conflict. Police may enforce this guideline in the same manner as above guideline.
3. Indoor gatherings of more than 10 are banned in restaurants, gyms, supermarkets, and general public places. Any place that holds a liquor licence is exempt from these rulings.
4. It is common courtesy to trust any citizen who holds an alcoholic beverage is considered to be Australian, therefore they should receive a handshake by any other passing Australian citizens.

The Department of Health also provided a statement outlining their decisions.

“The Department of Health strongly believes in the words of the Prime Minister and is ashamed by the reaction from the Australian public over the past months of this pandemic. The guidelines will not only allow for flexibility for those who choose to act rude and shamefully, but also allow for true Australians to act as if the pandemic did not exist.” As stated by the Minister of Health.

“We still encourage the usage of masks and gloves, as well as other PPE, but handshakes and hugs should be mandatory.”

The Opposition Leader has strongly condemned the actions of the Prime Minister and the Minister of health, releasing a series of tweets.

“The Prime Minister is completely unhinged and petty. He is dangerous. He is untrustworthy. He is deranged”

“The Prime Minister is failing to protect the lives in this Nation, and is choosing his insignificant moral views over the millions of lives at stake now”

“Department of Health can go cop one as well. They are consistently siding with the Prime Minister so they can push their own agendas on Abortion and illegalising it.”

In the final tweet, the Opposition Leader in referring to the ongoing efforts of the Minister for Health to illegalise abortions altogether again, reverting the laws set in place by states across the country during 2017 and 2018.

“In the upcoming election, I vow to remove these guidelines and create a new program teaching what it is really like to be ‘Australian’. The Prime Minister is dated and does not understand what modern Australians are like. The Guidelines completely isolate religious people, especially Muslims, as they’re prohibited from drinking alcohol. Is that fair? Is the Prime Minister a racist against Muslims?” as stated by the Opposition Leader in an inquiry via email.

After announced, the Guidelines were met with mixed reactions. Supporters of the Prime Minister included citizens who worked in hospitality, especially bars and pubs, as well as suppliers and farmers.

“Bars and Pubs being open is killa. It’s been months since I’ve been able to have a beer with the blokes, I miss em heaps. I’d rather get this COVID sh*t than not be able to have a drink on a Friday arvo. I think the Prime Minister is a good lad who understands what the country needs; the old ways back.”, said by Hank, the owner of the Shaw’s Bar near Goolwa.

On the opposing side, public service workers such as nurses and the police are heavily opposed to the changes and agree with the opposition on what should be done about it.

“The guidelines gobsmacked all of us here at the Force. We cannot fathom how the Prime Minister believed that this would bring not only safety from COVID-19 to the citizens, but safety in general from drunken idiots who terrorize the public. Sucker punch deaths have become non-existent over the past couple of months, and drunk driving is the lowest ever since 2008. These guidelines will revert all of the improvements seen from the lack of alcohol sales in bars and pubs.” as written by the Police Commissioner of South Australia on the SAPOL Facebook account.

“We have chosen to not follow the guidelines, and will remove patrons from bars and pubs, as well caution anyone seen hugging or handshaking, end of story. Don’t be an idiot.”

Nurses form across the country shared the resentment from the Police, with one passionate Nurse creating a change.org petition to revert the guidelines. When asked to interview, she refused, however her change.org petition information section provides her perspective colourfully.

“F*CK THE PRIME MINISTER. F*CK THE MINISTER OF HEALTH. F*CK ANYONE WHO FOLLOWS THEIR SH*TTY GUIDELINES. SAVE OUR ELDERS. SAVE OUR NATION FROM THIS TYRANY”

In the following weeks, The Prime Minister will be holding a press conference on vaccines and policy changes. With his views on COVID-19, it is assumed he will be taking an Anti-Vaccine stance.
